Consumer Protection Act

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019, provides for the establishment of the Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA) and Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions. It aims to protect consumers from unfair trade practices, ensure the right to be informed, and provide a mechanism for redressal of consumer grievances.

IPC Section 420

Cheating and Dishonestly Inducing Delivery of Property

This section may be applicable in cases of consumer fraud, where individuals or businesses engage in deceptive practices to cheat consumers and induce them to part with their money or property.
